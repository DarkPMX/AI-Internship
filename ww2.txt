1939: The war begins when Germany, under the leadership of Adolf Hitler, invades Poland

December 1941: Japan conducts a surprise attack on the U.S. naval base at Pearl Harbor in Hawaii

December 1941: Immediately following the attack on Pearl Harbor, the United States officially joins the war as part of the Allied powers

June 1944: The D-Day invasion takes place, where Allied soldiers land on the beaches of France. This crucial battle allows the Allies to begin pushing the German army back toward Germany

May 1945: Germany officially surrenders to the Allied forces, bringing an end to the war in Europe

August 1945: To end the continuing conflict in the Pacific, the United States drops two atomic bombs on the cities of Hiroshima and Nagasaki

September 2, 1945: Following the immense destruction caused by the atomic bombs, Japan surrenders, and World War II officially ends

Post-War: In the aftermath of the conflict, the United Nations is created to facilitate international cooperation, maintain peace, and prevent future world wars
